Ghana

Republic of Ghana
Gateway to Africa · Akwaaba!

The first sub-Saharan African country to gain independence in 1957, Ghana is the proud home of Kente cloth, the Golden Stool of the Ashanti, Highlife music, Cape Coast Castle, and Kwame Nkrumah's pan-African vision. With the most welcoming people in West Africa and the famous Akwaaba (welcome) spirit, Ghana has become the continent's model of democracy and cultural pride — and in December, the world's biggest party destination.

The big picture

Overview of Ghana

90+ groups · One nation

Ethnic Groups

Ghana has six major ethnic families. The Akan are the largest, comprising nearly half the population. The diversity of cultures, languages, and traditions makes Ghana one of West Africa's richest cultural tapestries.

~80 languages spoken

Languages of Ghana

Twi is so dominant in Ghana that even non-Akan Ghanaians often learn it as a second indigenous language. English is official, but Twi is the de facto national lingua franca — understood by over 80% of Ghanaians.

Soulful · Aromatic · Communal

Food & Cuisine

Ghanaian cuisine is built on fermented starches, rich soups, and the chop bar culture. The most defining feature is the "swallow" tradition — dough-like starches paired with boldly flavoured soups and stews. The best food is always at a chop bar run by Auntie.

Kente · Ankara · Batakari

Dressing & Fashion

Ghanaian fashion is anchored by Kente cloth and Adinkra fabric — not just clothes, but coded communication systems. Every pattern and colour carries specific philosophical meaning. Ghana's fashion identity is among the most distinctive on the continent.

Visual philosophy

Adinkra Symbols

Adinkra is a visual language of over 60 symbols, each representing a philosophical concept, proverb, or value. Developed by the Akan of Ghana — now one of Africa's most globally recognized symbol systems.

Family · Community · Libation

Marriage Traditions

Marriage in Ghana is a community affair — both families must meet, agree, and celebrate together. The universal first step is the Knocking Ceremony. Libation to ancestors must be poured before any important discussion begins.

Nyame · Vodu · Ubuntu

Beliefs & Spirituality

Ghana is predominantly Christian (~71%), with Islam (~18%) dominant in the north and traditional beliefs deeply woven into daily life. The saying goes: "Ghanaians pray in church on Sunday, pour libation on Monday, and consult the traditional healer when neither works."

Harvest · Royal · Sacred · Arts

Festivals & Celebrations

Ghana's festivals are among the most extraordinary in Africa — from the Homowo's ancestral feast to the Akwasidae's royal splendour. Every one is a living connection between the present and the ancient past.

Highlife · Hiplife · Afrobeats

Music & Pop Culture

Ghana is Africa's Highlife homeland — the genre born here in the 1920s gave rise to much of West Africa's modern music. From E.T. Mensah to Reggie Rockstone to Black Sherif — Ghana has shaped African sound for over 100 years.

13th century to present

History of Ghana

From the ancient Ashanti Empire and Cape Coast slave trade, through Kwame Nkrumah's triumphant independence speech in 1957, to the Year of Return in 2019 — Ghana's history is one of Africa's most remarkable stories.

16 Regions (expanded from 10 in 2019)

Administrative Regions

Ghana reorganized from 10 to 16 regions in 2019, creating 6 new regions to bring governance closer to the people.

Wisdom of the ancestors

Proverbs & Wisdom

Ghanaian proverbs — especially Akan proverbs in Twi — are among the most philosophically rich in Africa. Sankofa, Ubuntu, and Gye Nyame are known the world over.

The land of Akwaaba

Travel & Places

Ghana is one of West Africa's most accessible and welcoming travel destinations — consistently ranked among the continent's safest countries. The 2019 Year of Return transformed Ghana's global tourism profile forever.
