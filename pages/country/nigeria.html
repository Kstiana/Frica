Nigeria

Federal Republic of Nigeria
Giant of Africa

Africa's most populous nation and largest economy — Nigeria is the beating heart of Afrobeats music, Nollywood cinema, and the ancient civilizations of the Yoruba, Igbo, and Hausa-Fulani peoples. Home to 220 million people across 250+ ethnic groups, speaking 520+ languages — Nigeria is not just a country. It is a continent within a continent.

The big picture

Overview of Nigeria

250+ groups · One nation

Ethnic Groups

Nigeria has over 250 ethnic groups speaking 520+ languages. The three largest — Hausa-Fulani, Yoruba, and Igbo — account for approximately 60–70% of the population. Each has its own civilization, history, language, and cultural world.

Most linguistically diverse in Africa

Languages of Nigeria

Nigeria has over 520 living indigenous languages — the most of any country in Africa. English is the official language, but Nigerian Pidgin (Naija) is the de facto national tongue spoken by 75+ million.

Bold, aromatic, communal

Food & Cuisine

Nigerian cuisine is one of Africa's richest — built on palm oil, crayfish, fermented locust beans, and an enormous variety of leafy vegetables. Every ethnic group has its own distinct culinary tradition.

Coded communication in cloth

Dressing & Fashion

Nigerian fashion is one of the most vibrant and globally influential in Africa. From hand-woven Aso-Oke to bold Ankara prints, from the flowing Agbada to the regal Isiagu — Nigerian traditional attire carries deep cultural meaning.

Family · Community · Ceremony

Marriage Traditions

Marriage in Nigeria is fundamentally a union of two families, not just two individuals. The traditional ceremony is the most culturally significant. Bride price is universal — a token of respect and appreciation, not a purchase.

Spiritual richness

Beliefs & Spirituality

Nigeria is roughly equally divided between Christianity (~50%) and Islam (~50%), with indigenous traditional beliefs remaining vital — often practiced alongside both major religions.

Sacred · Royal · Communal

Festivals & Celebrations

Nigeria's festivals are among Africa's most extraordinary — from the Durbar's cavalry splendour to the sacred depth of Osun-Osogbo. Every one is a world of its own.

The beat of Africa

Music & Pop Culture

Nigeria is the undisputed music capital of Africa. Afrobeats — the global genre born from Lagos — has conquered the world's major music charts and brought African music to billions of listeners.

500 BCE to present

History of Nigeria

Nigeria's history stretches back thousands of years — from the Nok terracotta culture to the empires of Oyo and Benin, the Sokoto Caliphate, British colonization, independence, and the 4th Republic.

36 States + FCT

Administrative Divisions

Nigeria is organized into 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory (Abuja), grouped into 6 geopolitical zones.

Wisdom of the ancestors

Proverbs & Wisdom

Nigerian proverbs are not just sayings — they are compressed philosophy, passed down through generations as the highest form of cultural teaching.

Beyond the postcards

Travel & Places

Nigeria has some of West Africa's most compelling destinations — from ancient kingdoms to modern megacities, rainforests, savannas, and a stunning coastline.
